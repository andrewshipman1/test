01 · Brand Idea

Every competitor reaches for abstraction. Parcel goes the other way: total confidence in the literal. Manhattan developers spend their careers thinking about parcels. The name says we speak your language without decoration. That directness, at this price point, is its own form of authority.

Core positioning PARCEL · NYC · v1
THE PARCEL
BEFORE THE LISTING.
EVERY WEEK.
FOR MANHATTAN.

The brand argument

Directness reads as confidence at this price point. Bloomberg is named after a person. CoStar is named after what it does. A developer hears "Parcel" and knows immediately. No translation needed. The visual identity and voice carry the premium signal the name doesn't supply on its own.

Vs. the field

CoStar and Reonomy are databases. They answer what you ask. Parcel tells you what to ask for — then tells you who owns it, how long they've held it, what the zoning allows that they're not building, and why now is the moment. The data isn't the differentiation. The score is.

Bronze rule

Bronze appears only on scores ≥ 85, ownership-change flags, and the active parcel pin. Hardware on a building — structural, not decorative.

Grain rule

Grain lives on marketing surfaces only. The product UI is clean drafting. The grain marks the threshold between brochure and tool.

Patina rule

Patina is for confirmed states only — a saved parcel, a completed action. It reads as age and permanence, not optimism. Not success-green.

05 · Brand Voice

Parcel's voice is a senior partner at a legendary architecture firm describing a site. No enthusiasm. No selling. The confidence is in the facts, and the facts are chosen carefully. One thought per line. Never more than necessary.

Not this
"Discover amazing AI-powered insights and unlock your next Manhattan acquisition opportunity today!"
This
"215 W 98th. R8A. FAR 6.02 unused. Owner since 2003. Score: 94."
Not this
"Our powerful platform helps real estate investors find the best development opportunities."
This
"The parcel before the listing. Every week, for Manhattan."
Not this
"Sign up today and start finding deals with our easy-to-use interface!"
This
"Parcel is invite-only. Apply."

We say

PARCEL IDENTIFY SCORE SIGNAL QUALIFY ACQUISITION

We never say

DISCOVER UNLOCK INSIGHTS POWERFUL LEVERAGE PROPERTY

Tone attributes

Lapidary — carved, not written Factual — no flourish Terse — one thought per line Institutional — permanent weight Expert — earns its authority

Go-to-market posture

Invite-only. No self-serve sign-up. A sparse marketing site with a single CTA: Apply. Applicants are reviewed. The buyer should feel like they were selected, not that they signed up.

Digital touchpoints

No social. Referral-first. Name does the work.

07 · The Two Surfaces

The brand has exactly two surface modes. Understanding the boundary between them is the single most important design decision.

Marketing surface

Grain · Ivory Lt · Barlow 200 · Playfair allowed
PARCEL

Marketing site, email digest, invite letters, printed collateral. Grain texture. Serif pull-quotes allowed. All-caps condensed display type.

Product UI surface

No grain · Concrete · Barlow 300 · Mono for data
94

Authenticated app only. No grain, no serif, pure data. Clean drafting. This is the working document, not the brochure.

12 · Component Signoff Checklist

Before marking any component or screen done, confirm each item below.

Font is Barlow Condensed (or IBM Plex Mono for all numeric data values)
Background is Concrete (#252220) for product, or Ivory Lt (#F4EEE5) for marketing — nothing else
No rounded corners anywhere — border-radius: 0
No box shadows — depth through borders only
Bronze (#A8824E) appears only on scores ≥85 or signal flags — not decoratively
All numeric values are IBM Plex Mono weight 300
All metadata labels are ALL CAPS, 10px, tracking 0.20em, IBM Plex Mono or Barlow Condensed
Grain (.p-grain) is absent from product UI — marketing only
Playfair Display is absent from product UI — marketing and digest only
Title block motif is present in footer / attribution area of any full-page view
UI copy is terse and factual — no marketing language inside the authenticated product
Patina (#8E9E8A) used only for confirmed/saved state — not as a success color
Brand mark is sourced from parcel-assets.svg — not redrawn
