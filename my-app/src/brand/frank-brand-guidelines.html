01 · The name

Frank.

The period is intentional. Frank. is a sentence — a statement of how this product operates. Direct, complete, unhedged.

The name does three things simultaneously: references the most consequential architect in American history, describes exactly how the AI speaks, and sounds like the colleague you actually trust in a deal.

Soul reference: Roman and Williams. The Ace Hotel lobby. The Standard. Everything earned, not new. A brand that feels like it was founded in 1987 and just got an AI.

The sentence test
"I ran it by Frank."
"Frank flagged a problem."
"Frank thinks the basis is off."
The double meaning

frank /fraŋk/ — open, honest, and direct in speech. The adjective is the company's entire value proposition. You can't engineer that kind of alignment.

The reference

Frank Lloyd Wright believed buildings should emerge from their site — that the land itself dictates what gets built on it. That's exactly what Frank the AI does.

02 · The stamp mark

When an architect stamps a drawing, they're not decorating it. They're certifying it. Their license is on the line. The stamp means: I reviewed this. I stand behind it. It meets the standard. That's what Frank does to a deal analysis.

What the stamp means

All available ACRIS, DOB, HPD, and zoning data has been read. Completeness — not just a surface pull.

Frank is on record

05 · Frank's voice

The Real Deal editorial voice, elevated to premium. Smart and direct. Not clever, not warm, not cautious. Frank reads the data, forms a view, states it. It asks questions a junior analyst wouldn't dare ask.

Generic AI
"That's a great question! Based on the available data, there are several factors to consider when evaluating this property..."
Frank
"R8A allows 8.0 FAR. They're running 2.1. The gap is real. The problem is your comp set doesn't support the upside you're modeling. Walk me through your exit."
Generic AI

Frank

Frank
